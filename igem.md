**What is your goal with iGEM Toronto? In other words, why do you want to join iGEM Toronto? [1000 characters max]**

For the longest time, I could not understand how or why neural networks worked because it never appeared intuitive to me how a series of nodes connected together has anything to do with intelligent behaviour. In preparation for the racing team interview, I had to tackle this challenge head-on as I wanted to impress the team lead. I learned that networks take an input. The input can vary, but most notably, an array of 784 nodes in grayscale that serves as the image. Then, the input flows down the layers, which initially have random weights, eventually reaching its destination, where it should light up a node with a corresponding feature (e.g. a t-shirt). Then, using a loss function, we can determine how far off the prediction was, backpropagate the error, and create a better network. I'm particularly proud that I'm able to understand neural networks now and explain them effortlessly to others and I hope to continue learning and achieving mastery over more difficult tasks in the future.
